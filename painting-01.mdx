There is a quality of light at 5pm in January that I keep returning to. Not golden hour — earlier than that. A lateral grey that flattens everything and makes familiar objects look like they're being seen for the first time.

This painting began there. At the edge of a table. The corner of a window. The way a glass of water holds the room's colour without announcing it.

I am not trying to render the scene. I am trying to stay long enough with it that something true comes through.

---

The linen was prepared with a warm mid-tone ground — a mixture of raw umber and titanium white, thin enough to feel the texture underneath. The first session was entirely about placement. Where the weight sits. Where the eye rests.

Painting is the slowest thinking I know how to do.
